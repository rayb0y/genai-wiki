# Interviews: Synthesis

The rolling synthesis of every interview in this section. Each interview is filed once on its own page, built from [[interview-template]]. This page accumulates the themes that show up across them, so you do not have to re-read transcripts to remember what was found. It is updated whenever a new interview is ingested.

## Interviews

- [[interviewee-1]] (about 72 min): a design and innovation professional on what makes teams click or stall, where her real thinking happens (out loud, with a sounding board), and where AI helps or hurts in innovation work.

## Themes across interviews

These are preliminary, drawn from a single interview so far. They are starting hypotheses to confirm or revise as more interviews are added, not established patterns.

- Stuckness comes from missing shared purpose, not missing skill. The worst team had no unifying goal; the best teams shared one, sometimes an external "common villain."
- The unblocking mechanism people value is a sounding board: thinking out loud and hearing a different view restructures their thinking and breaks circular thinking. This is the project's central bet about AI-as-questioner, stated unprompted.
- AI is trusted as a grounded resource (absorbing and confirming facts from your own documents) and distrusted for two things: validation (it praises) and idea generation (it aggregates the popular rather than inventing the new).
- Verification is the recurring worry: unverified AI output becomes a "shaky foundation" others build on.
- Trust requirements named for an AI tool: transparency/traceability, correct version and context handling, no hallucination, and no sycophantic flattery.

## Open questions

- Does a well-timed question actually unblock teams, and can AI deliver it, or only a human? (The core hypothesis.)
- Where in the iNPD phases do these deadlocks land? Map each interview's stuck moments to a phase.
- What does a non-flattering, source-tied AI questioner need to do to be trusted?
- Run more interviews before treating any theme above as a pattern.

The themes above connect to established research in [[team-dynamics]] (psychological safety, shared purpose, collective intelligence, transactive memory, conflict types).
